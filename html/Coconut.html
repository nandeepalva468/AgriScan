Coconut Diseases & Symptoms

Symptoms

Early-stage infestation is often seen as small, pale triangular zones near the perianth. These areas eventually turn brown and expand as the coconut develops. In advanced stages, the fruit surface becomes corky with deep longitudinal cracks, often intersected by horizontal ones. The brown discoloration is especially visible before the coconut matures.

Cause

The primary cause is the infestation of the coconut mite (Aceria guerreronis). These mites invade the area under the perianth of young coconuts and damage the developing tissues. Environmental stress and mechanical injuries may also contribute to similar symptoms.

Description

Coconut mite is a major pest in coconut-producing regions. It feeds on the soft, developing tissues of young fruits, causing scarring and poor nut development. As the infestation progresses, visible surface damage becomes prominent. The issue may often be confused with physical injuries or environmental stress, such as cold or wind.

Management

Regular monitoring of young coconuts is essential. Use of miticides or neem-based sprays can help control infestation. Maintaining proper spacing between trees improves air circulation. Remove and destroy heavily infested coconuts to reduce mite populations. Biological control using predatory mites is also being researched as an eco-friendly solution.
