Arecanut Diseases & Symptoms

Symptoms

Rotting and extensive shedding of immature nuts is commonly observed. Dark green or yellowish lesions appear near the calyx. Affected nuts lose their natural green luster and become brown or shriveled. White fungal growth may be visible on fallen nuts and inflorescence. In some cases, “dry Mahali” occurs—rotting without premature nut drop, especially late in the season.

Cause

The disease is primarily caused by Phytophthora palmivora, a fungal-like organism (oomycete) that thrives in humid, wet conditions. It spreads through rain splash, wind, and contaminated tools or soil.

Description

Fruit rot is one of the most destructive diseases affecting arecanut plantations. It appears mostly during the monsoon season. Infected nuts develop wet, soft rot and fall prematurely. The inflorescence and fruit stalks may also be affected, leading to poor nut formation and yield loss.

Management

Use preventive sprays like Bordeaux mixture (1%) or copper oxychloride. Ensure good field drainage and avoid water stagnation. Collect and destroy fallen nuts and inflorescence to prevent disease buildup. Maintain proper spacing and prune to allow better airflow. Apply fungicides before monsoon and repeat at 30-day intervals if needed.
