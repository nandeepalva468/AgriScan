Paddy Diseases & Symptoms

Symptoms

The disease can affect paddy at all growth stages and all aerial parts, especially leaves, neck, and nodes. It starts as small specks on leaves that enlarge into spindle-shaped spots (0.5–1.5 cm long) with ashy centers. These lesions often merge into large, irregular patches, causing leaf drying and yield loss. Neck and node infections lead to blackening and breaking of panicles, reducing grain formation.

Cause

The primary cause is the fungus Magnaporthe oryzae, responsible for rice blast disease. It spreads through windborne spores and thrives in humid environments with poor air circulation and excessive nitrogen fertilizer use.

Description

Rice blast, also known as rotten neck or rice fever, is one of the most destructive diseases in rice-growing areas. It was first recorded in India in 1918 and is now reported in over 80 rice-growing countries. Yield loss can range from 20% to as high as 70–80% in severe outbreaks.

Management

Use resistant rice varieties and follow proper crop rotation. Apply balanced fertilizers — avoid excessive nitrogen. Use seed treatments with fungicides and apply systemic fungicides like tricyclazole or isoprothiolane at early disease stages. Ensure good water management, proper spacing, and timely weeding to improve air circulation and reduce humidity.
